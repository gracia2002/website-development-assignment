Our Instructors

Mauricio Gomez

DoBu Martial Arts is run by Mauricio Gomez, who also serves as its head martial arts instructor. He has more than 15 years of experience competing in international tournaments and holds a 4th Dan Black Belt in Judo, a 3rd Dan Black Belt in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, and a 1st Dan Black Belt in Karate. Mauricio is an IFMA-certified Muay Thai coach with expertise in ground techniques, hitting combos, and customised competition preparation. Discipline, respect, and pushing boundaries on and off the mat are the cornerstones of his teaching philosophy.

Sarah Nova

Sarah Nova, a 5th Dan Black Belt in Karate (recognised by the WKF) and a National Karate Champion (2018, 2020), works as an assistant martial arts coach. Sarah is an expert in women's self-defence classes, precision hitting, and kata (forms), and she is certified to teach children's self-defence. Karate, in her opinion, helps pupils of all ages find their strengths by encouraging focus and self-assurance

Guy Victory

Guy Victory, an assistant martial arts teacher who is a first Dan Black Belt in Judo and a second Dan Black Belt in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (IBJJF). Guy, a former MMA fighter with a 5-1 amateur record, is an expert in no-gi Jiu-Jitsu, takedown defence, and submission grappling. Comparing Jiu-Jitsu to a game of chess, he emphasises that it is more about strategy and flexibility than strength.

Morris Davis

Morris Davis holds a 3rd Dan Black Belt in Karate, is an approved Muay Thai coach (UKMF-certified), and works as an assistant martial arts coach. Morris, a combat sports coach with more than 10 years of expertise, emphasises cardio-intensive pad training, elbow/knee strikes, and clinch work. He calls Muay Thai "the art of eight limbs" and stresses learning the fundamentals, saying that for those who practise hard, the ring becomes a playground.

Trad Santiago

DoBu's fitness instructor, Trad Santiago, is qualified in health and nutrition (NASM) and has a Bachelor of Science in Sports Science. Trad creates nutrition planning, agility exercises, and injury prevention programs for weight-class competitors, with a focus on combat athletes' strength and conditioning. "Your body is your weapon; train it smart and fuel it right" is his motto, which symbolises his all-encompassing approach to performance.

Harpreet Kaur

Fitness instructor Harpreet Kaur is an ACSM-certified rehabilitation specialist who holds a Bachelor of Science in Physiotherapy and a Master of Science in Sports Science. She emphasises joint prehab, flexibility and mobility training (including yoga for fighters), and post-injury recovery. Harpreet emphasises conscious body care and feels that movement is medicine, whether for healing or combat readiness
